"

From a passing comment to a prototype in 3 days.

It started at a cross-country carnival. I was watching my daughter run when I bumped into one of her friend's mums. She mentioned she'd bought a building set for her nephew and was disappointed it arrived in nothing but plain brown packaging. "I really wish somebody would just build a box that looks like a brick," she said. "If you make it, you owe me 10%."

She was laughing. I was already thinking.

An hour later my brain hadn't stopped processing. Within two hours we had the name. Within four hours we had a website. By hour five we had engineering schematics and a plan for how the box would actually work. At seven hours I was standing in Officeworks buying materials for the first prototype.

Three days later, BRIXBOX was sitting on my kitchen table.

Some ideas take years. This one took a conversation, a carnival, and a sheet of cardboard.

— Jay, founder of theviking.io

3D Pop-Up Preview
The pop-up stud uses alternating valley and mountain folds in a radial starburst pattern. When pushed from beneath, the scored segments rise and lock into a 3D form. The octagon distributes fold stress across 16 score lines — each bending just 22°.
Base Tray — Die-Line Net
Assembly Sequence
The base tray folds up from a single cross-shaped blank. Four walls, four corner tabs, one flat base. The lid is identical but slightly larger, with integrated pop-up studs on the top panel. Both ship flat and assemble in seconds.
BRIXBOX Wordmark — Construction Grid
The BRIXBOX wordmark uses a stencil typeface — deliberately chosen because stencils are how the real boxes get branded. The segmented letterforms reference the gaps in a physical stencil cut, connecting the product's manufacturing process to its visual identity. Applied via spray stencil on kraft cardboard.
